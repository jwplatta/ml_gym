# Referrals and Final Interviews
## Question
An HR team records whether applicants came from a referral and whether they were invited to a final interview.

|                      | Final Interview (F) | No Final Interview (¬F) | Total |
|----------------------|---------------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Referred (R)         | 30                  | 20                       | 50    |
| Not Referred (¬R)    | 18                  | 32                       | 50    |
| **Total**            | **48**              | **52**                   | **100** |

Find all three probabilities:
1. P(R)
2. P(F)
3. P(R ∩ F)
## Solution
Since the total is 100, each probability is the corresponding count divided by 100.

P(R) = 50/100 = 0.50

P(F) = 48/100 = 0.48

P(R ∩ F) = 30/100 = 0.30

**Answers:**
- P(R) = 0.50
- P(F) = 0.48
- P(R ∩ F) = 0.30

This problem reinforces that marginals come from row or column totals, while joint probabilities come from a single interior cell.
